Random thoughts from Pycon Dublin

Oct 13, 2014 Christophe Guerrier

Over the weekend I attended my first Pycon in Dublin, Ireland. It is organised by the wonderful crowd of Python Ireland (http://python.ie/), I would actually recommend that you go to one of their monthly meetups if you have any interest in the Python language.

Main stage at Pycon 2014

I was not sure what to expect, but the experience turned out to be truly enjoyable, and I finished up the weekend, my head full of ideas to investigate with python.

Here's my favourite highlights of the weekend, in no particular order.
  • Ian Ozsvald (@ianozsvald): The Real Unsolved Problems in Data Science
    You can read it there:
    http://ianozsvald.com/2014/10/11/my-keynote-at-pyconireland-2014-the-real-unsolved-problems-in-data-science/

  • A lightning talk by @saoili about getting into python jobs, which comfort me in my current chosen path.
    For those who were sensibly asleep when I tweeted this at 2am, my #pyconie lightning talk slides: http://t.co/xTvf1Y2k4e

    — Saoilí (@saoili) October 12, 2014

  • On the same lightning talk show, a very entertaining demonstration of failing at data science (Homeless cloud by an engineer from rehabstudios, I forgot the name), but also a silent presentation by @dontusethiscode.

  • Rachel Willmer (@rwillmer) in her keynote: "A Tech Person's Adventures in Startup", gave me some inspiration and food for thoughts on creating your own company.

  • I realize that Python was widely used in the financial industry (something I did not know before ) thanks to Dr. Yves J. Hilpisch (@dyjh) keynote : "Python and the Financial Industry".

  • Finally a very cool demonstration, inside iPython notebook on how to build an earth centered universe by Brandon Rhodes (@brandon_rhodes).

Overall fun, exciting and energizing conference, I will probably be back next year, more engaged.
